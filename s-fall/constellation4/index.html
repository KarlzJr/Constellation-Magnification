Aries Constellation

Best seen in December around 9 p.m., Aries is a simple constellation composed of only four stars, but can be easily found if the stargazer can find Orion’s belt. Orion’s belt is made up of three stars in a straight line. Once found, extend an imaginary straight line from the belt that continues to Aldebaran, a bright star in the Taurus constellation. Continuing from the imaginary straight line is the Pleiades, a group of seven stars closely bunched together. Next to this bunch should be a bright star called Hamal, which makes up Aires.
Greeks and the Egyptians traditionally associated the constellation with a ram. In Greek mythology, the ram was golden and was sacrificed to Zeus, the Greek leader of the gods (space.com).
For a more experienced star gazer, use the information below in finding the constellation:
  • Right Ascension: 3 hours
  • Declination: 20 degrees
  • Visible between latitudes 90 degrees and -60 degrees
