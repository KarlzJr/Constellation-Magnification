Virgo Constellation

Best seen in May at 9 p.m, Virgo is a more simple constellation to spot in the night sky. First look for the Big Dipper. West of the Big Dipper, there is a constellation called Bootes that contains a yellowish, bright star called Arcturus. To find Arcturus, just simply imagine the curve of the Big Dipper handle to be extended until you find the midway point of Arcturus. Continue tracing or imagining the handle curvature from Arcturus, you will find Spica, the brightest star in Virgo. It resembles the left hand of the constellation, and continue looking a bit Northeast of Spica for a bright star called Porrima which is the heart of the constellation.
The constellation Virgo is usually associated with the Greek goddess of justice, Dike. Dike was the daughter of Zeus and Greek Titaness Themis. Virgo is usually depicted with angel-like wings, with an ear of wheat in her left hand, marked by the bright star Spica. (constellation-guide.com)
For a more experienced star gazer, use the information below in finding the constellation:
  • Right Ascension: 13 hours
  • Declination: 0 degrees
  • Visible between latitudes 80 and minus 80 degrees
